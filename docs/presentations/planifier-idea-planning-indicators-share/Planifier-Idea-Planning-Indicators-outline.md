## Slide 2

- IDEA PLANNING CANVAS
- PLANNING PRINCIPLE
- A new idea should enter as a testable question, not a prediction.
- The product should keep the user focused on evidence, invalidation, and review. That makes future saved trades useful instead of just stored screenshots.
- Market
- Which pair, stock, or index is being studied?
- Chart read
- Trend, level, current price location.
- Indicator clue
- What context does the indicator add?
- Practice rule
- Entry idea, target, risk, invalidation.
- Review
- What happened and what did I learn?
- Idea prompt
- I am studying [market] on [timeframe] because price is near [level]. The indicator says [context]. I am wrong if [condition].
- Quality check
- Can I explain it in one sentence? Can I show the level on the chart? Do I know where the idea fails?
- Product implication
- The builder should ask fewer vague questions and more chart-specific ones.

## Slide 5

- INDICATOR CARDS

- This keeps learning mode useful without repeating the same sentence before every line of text.
- Measures
- What the indicator is actually calculating in plain English.
- Useful when
- The market condition where this indicator helps most.
- Watch out
- Where it fails, lags, or creates false confidence.

## Slide 6

- INDICATOR 01 - MOVING AVERAGES
- MOVING AVERAGES
- Use MA/EMA to explain trend direction, slope, and pullback context.
- Best beginner use: is price above/below the average, and is the average rising or falling?
- 96.80
- resistance
- support
- 20 EMA
- 50 SMA
- What it measures
- Average price over a chosen number of candles. EMA reacts faster than SMA.
- Beginner use
- Trend context: rising average plus pullback can be cleaner than chasing a candle after it already moved.
- Common trap
- Moving averages lag. A crossover after a big move can be late evidence, not a fresh idea.

## Slide 7

- INDICATOR 02 - RSI
- RSI
- Use RSI to study momentum, exhaustion, and divergence without treating 70/30 as automatic.
- Best beginner use: ask whether momentum agrees with the price move near a real level.
- 96.80
- resistance
- support
- 70 overbought area
- 30 oversold area
- What it measures
- Recent momentum: how strong gains are compared with losses.
- Good question
- Is price making a new high while RSI is weaker? That can warn momentum is fading.
- Common trap
- RSI can stay high in strong uptrends and low in strong downtrends. It is context, not a reversal button.

- 96.80

## Slide 9

- INDICATORS 05-06 - MACD + ATR
- MACD + ATR
- Use MACD for momentum shifts and ATR for volatility-aware plan sizing.
- These indicators are stronger as planning tools than entry permission tools.
- MACD: momentum shift
- Measures the relationship between fast and slow moving averages. Beginner use: watch whether momentum is expanding or fading near a level. Trap: late crossovers after price already moved.
- ATR: volatility and room
- Measures average candle range. Beginner use: avoid placing the wrong-if line so tight that normal volatility knocks it out. Trap: ATR does not predict direction.
- ATR RANGE
- Product idea
- Indicator cards should show what it measures, when it helps, when it fails, and one beginner question tied to the current chart.
